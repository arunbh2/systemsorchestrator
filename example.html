SELCO Foundation: System Orchestration

Building Sustainable Ecosystems for Underserved Communities

Leveraging Energy as a Catalyst for Development

Who is SELCO Foundation?

  • Mission: Create solutions using sustainable energy to bridge environmental sustainability and poverty alleviation [cite: 23].
  • Goal: Foster sustainable development via decentralized renewable energy (DRE) [cite: 24, 25].
  • Values: Rooted in equity, inclusivity, transparency, and sustainability [cite: 28, 29, 30, 7].
  • Approach: Respects local contexts, uses participatory methods, focuses on economic empowerment and need-based innovation [cite: 7, 31, 32, 15].

What is System Orchestration?

  • Definition: Uniting diverse stakeholders, aligning efforts, and sustaining progress towards shared goals [cite: 1, 6, 101].
  • SELCO's Role: Acts as a central coordinating entity [cite: 4]; a 'conductor' guiding stakeholders (govt., finance, tech providers, NGOs, communities) [cite: 1, 7, 103].
  • Focus: Propelling collective action, building bridges, empowering local leaders, and maximizing impact [cite: 11, 12, 105].
  • Key Function: Fosters transformational change by weaving together actors and galvanizing collective action across ecosystems [cite: 7].

The Ecosystem Approach

  • Core Idea: Sustainable impact requires a supportive environment (technology, finance, skills, markets), not just technology deployment [cite: 34, 35, 39, 170, 224].
  • Pillars: Integrates Technology (appropriate & efficient), Finance (inclusion & affordability), Entrepreneurship (local capacity & enterprises), and Policy (influencing for conducive environment) [cite: 36, 37, 38, 8, 12, 79, 756, 765, 785].
  • User-Centricity: Grounded in understanding end-user needs; solutions tailored to local contexts [cite: 8, 84, 85, 439].
  • Goal: Create self-sustaining systems that flourish beyond direct involvement [cite: 40, 41, 81].

Where It Works: Nexus Areas

  • Focus: Critical intersections like Health-Energy and Energy-Agriculture [cite: 2, 42, 59, 102, 161, 759, 753].
  • Health-Energy: Providing reliable solar power & efficient equipment to health facilities (e.g., 25,000 target) [cite: 43, 17, 727, 793].
    Impact Example: Reduced vaccine wastage (>80%), improved service quality, cost savings [cite: 57, 55, 56]. Solarizing boat clinics [cite: 58, 574, 580].
  • Energy-Agriculture: DRE solutions across the value chain for small farmers (irrigation, processing, cold storage) [cite: 27, 59, 60, 61, 754, 755].
    Impact Example: Empowering millet farmers [cite: 68, 27], solar cold storage reducing post-harvest loss [cite: 69, 70], hydroponic fodder stations [cite: 70].
  • Impact Goal: Drive systemic change for sustainable livelihoods and wellbeing in underserved communities [cite: 5, 108, 220, 750].
