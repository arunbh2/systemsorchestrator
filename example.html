Proposed System Orchestrator Model for Practical Action

Practical Action as the System Orchestrator:
Acting as a neutral convener and facilitator to foster collaboration and drive collective action towards a sustainable and equitable Agriculture-Energy Nexus in India[cite: 124].

Core Functions:

  • Convening Diverse Stakeholders[cite: 125]:
    • Leverage existing networks and reputation[cite: 125].
    • Bring together farmers, energy providers, policymakers, NGOs, private sector actors, and research institutions[cite: 125].
    • Create platforms for dialogue and interaction[cite: 126].
    • Foster mutual understanding and identify common ground[cite: 126].
  • Facilitating a Shared Vision and Agenda[cite: 127]:
    • Utilize participatory workshops, dialogues, and frameworks (e.g., Theory U)[cite: 127].
    • Collaboratively define challenges, identify priorities, and establish a common agenda[cite: 128].
  • Promoting Knowledge Sharing and Capacity Building[cite: 129]:
    • Act as a central hub for collecting, curating, and disseminating knowledge and best practices[cite: 129].
    • Facilitate capacity-building initiatives for stakeholders[cite: 130].
  • Supporting Pilot Projects and Innovation[cite: 131]:
    • Leverage experience in piloting and scaling solutions[cite: 131].
    • Support design, implementation, and evaluation of innovative projects[cite: 131].
    • Provide technical assistance, facilitate partnerships, and help secure funding[cite: 132].
  • Advocating for Integrated Policy Changes[cite: 133]:
    • Use insights from fieldwork and partners[cite: 133].
    • Advocate for supportive policy reforms at state and national levels[cite: 133].
    • Engage policymakers, present evidence-based recommendations, and build coalitions[cite: 134].
  • Fostering Market-Based Solutions[cite: 135]:
    • Facilitate the development and scaling of market-based solutions[cite: 135].
    • Connect farmers with the private sector[cite: 136].
    • Promote innovative business models[cite: 136].
    • Help create an enabling environment for sustainable technologies/practices[cite: 136].

Operational Mechanisms & Principles:

  • Guided by Practical Action's PMSD approach (systems thinking, participation, facilitation, gender equality)[cite: 137].
  • Aim for a temporary, catalytic role, empowering local actors for long-term ownership and sustainability[cite: 138].
